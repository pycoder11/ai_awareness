Policy Recommendations & Student Pledge

Suggested school policy (short version)

  1. Define allowed uses: brainstorming, outlines, citation help with explicit teacher approval.
  2. Define disallowed uses: submitting auto-generated text or code as original work.
  3. Verification expectations: students must show process work and sources for major assignments.
  4. Consequences: clear, proportional academic consequences and opportunities to learn (not just punish).
  5. AI literacy: mandatory lesson on verification, hallucinations, and ethical use.

Sample classroom requirement (for teachers)

For essay assignments: require a short process write-up (drafts, notes, and a paragraph explaining what was AI-assisted and what was student-generated). Teachers reserve the right to request oral explanation of key ideas.

Student Pledge — print, sign, or email

Green Level Responsible AI Pledge

I pledge to use AI tools responsibly: to learn, not to replace my thinking. I will disclose when AI helped me, verify AI-generated claims, and seek feedback to ensure I am learning the skills my assignments are meant to teach.

How you can help — Call to action

  • Students: Sign and share the pledge. Talk to your teacher about assignment guidelines.
  • Teachers: Adopt a short AI policy and require process evidence.
  • Administrators: Add AI literacy to your curriculum and require transparency for AI in grading/hiring tools.
