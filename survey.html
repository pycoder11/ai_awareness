Survey Results & Interview Highlights

Survey snapshot (n ≈ 200)

  • Most common uses: polishing emails, research help, studying aid.
  • Most common reason: save time / get tasks done quicker.
  • Concerns reported: becoming lazier, losing skills, over-reliance on AI.
  • Majority outcome: many still believe AI's benefits outweigh downsides — but many simultaneously worry about misuse.

Interpretation: the community values AI for convenience but lacks a shared framework for responsible use.

Interview excerpts

Murali — software veteran

“It sped up mundane tasks, but expertise is still paramount. AI provides noise; you still need technical skill to sift the signal.”

Ashwani — consultant

“AI is a tool to accelerate innovation, but high-level thinking should come from people.”

What the data suggests

There is a gap: users know the risks intellectually but may not have guidance on how to employ AI responsibly day-to-day. Closing that gap is where schools and workplaces must act.
