Is AI helping or hurting IT teams?

A short, direct look at how AI is changing work in IT companies. This site explains risks and offers simple, practical ideas for teams.

Read the Argument See the Evidence

Primary claim

AI can speed up work in computer science, but many teams use it without checks. This can harm learning, allow bad data to spread, and hide biased decisions. We should keep the benefits while reducing the harms.

Note

This site is focused on AI use inside IT companies and computer science teams. It is not aimed at schools or other settings.

Why it matters

Shortcuts can speed delivery. They can also stop people from building deep skills that matter for long term code quality and security.

See evidence →

Risks

Models can invent facts, leak private code, or make biased choices when used in hiring or review tools. Teams need ways to check results.

Read more →

Act

Adopt simple rules, teach verification, and require human review for major decisions.
