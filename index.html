Is AI helping or hurting society?

A student's urgent, evidence-backed case that AI must be guided — not allowed to replace how we think. From classrooms to workplaces, we must choose how to use this powerful tool.

Read the Argument Sign the Pledge

Primary Claim

The use of AI in society is harmful for the vast majority of us — not because the technology is inherently evil, but because unregulated use substitutes thinking for doing and erodes learning, fairness, and truth.

Personal Note

“I fed my history teacher’s prompt into an AI detector. Result: AI/GPT generated. My heart dropped.” — Adit Zetshi

Why it matters

AI shortcuts can make us faster in the short term and dumber in the long term. Students who outsource thinking miss practice in reasoning, creativity, and verification.

Risks

Hallucinations, biased automated decisions, and opaque workplace algorithms can harm individuals and communities.
